Flower Use For Medicine

Alpha Calendula

Alpha Calendula

Skin Healing: Calendula is perhaps best known for its skin-healing properties. It can be applied topically in the form of creams, ointments, or infused oils to soothe and promote the healing of minor cuts, burns, wounds, insect bites, and rashes.

Skin Conditions: Due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, Calendula is often used to alleviate symptoms associated with various skin conditions,dermatitis, and acne. It can help reduce redness, itching, and irritation associated with these conditions.

Echinacea Purpurea

Echinacea Purpurea

Cold and Flu Relief: Echinacea is commonly used to alleviate symptoms associated with the common cold, such as sore throat, cough, congestion, and fever. Some research suggests of cold symptoms may help shorten the duration of illness and reduce symptom severity.

Respiratory Health: Due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, Echinacea may help support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of conditions such as bronchitis, sinusitis, and upper respiratory infections.

Rosa

Rosa

Rose Water: Rose water, made by steeping rose petals in water, has been used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It's often used in skincare to calm irritated skin and reduce redness. It can also be ingested for its mild anti-inflammatory effects.

Rose Petal Tea: Rose petal tea is made by steeping dried rose petals in hot water. It's believed to have mild sedative properties, helping to promote relaxation and alleviate stress and anxiety. It's also sometimes used to relieve menstrual cramps.

Red Hibiscus

Red Hibiscus

Hibiscus Tea: One of the most common uses of hibiscus in medicine is as a herbal tea made from the dried calyces (the outer part of the flower). Hibiscus tea is rich in antioxidants, particularly flavonoids and anthocyanins.

Skincare: Hibiscus extracts are used in skincare products for their purported anti-aging and moisturizing properties. They may help promote collagen production, improve skin elasticity, and protect against environmental damage.

Sunflower

Sunflower

Skin Health: Sunflower oil is commonly used in skincare products due to its emollient properties. It can help moisturize the skin, improve skin barrier function, and soothe inflammation. It's often used in formulations for dry or sensitive skin.

Traditional Medicine: In traditional herbal medicine, sunflower petals have been used to make infusions or teas believed to have diuretic properties, promoting urine flow and potentially aiding in the treatment of urinary tract infections or kidney issues.

Lotus

Lotus

Digestive Health: Lotus seeds and rhizomes are believed to have digestive benefits. They may help alleviate gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea, indigestion, and bloating. Lotus seeds are sometimes included in herbal formulations to support overall digestive function.

Cardiovascular Health: Lotus contains compounds with potential cardiovascular benefits. It is believed to help regulate blood pressure and cholesterol levels. Lotus leaf extract, is used in traditional medicine to support heart health and circulation.
